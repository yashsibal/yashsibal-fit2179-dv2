# FIT2179 Data Visualisation 2 — How Australia Moves

Author: Yash Sibal  
Date: 3 June 2026

## Project summary

This project is a single-page scrolling data visualisation about Australian adult sport, fitness and physical activity participation. It uses Vega-Lite maps and diagrams to tell a story about the balance between traditional organised sport and flexible fitness/lifestyle activities.

## Main question

Is Australia still mainly a traditional sporting nation, or are flexible fitness activities now central to how Australians stay active?

## Data sources

1. Australian Sports Commission AusPlay Data Tables, January-December 2025, released 30 April 2026  
   URL: https://www.ausport.gov.au/clearinghouse/research/ausplay/results/data-tables

2. Australian Bureau of Statistics, National, state and territory population, September 2025  
   URL: https://www.abs.gov.au/statistics/people/population/national-state-and-territory-population/latest-release

3. Australia states GeoJSON by rowanhogan  
   URL: https://github.com/rowanhogan/australian-states

## Notes

- Adult means people aged 15 and over, following the AusPlay definition.
- Activity-level participation rates are not mutually exclusive because one person can participate in multiple activities.
- Estimated participant numbers are AusPlay estimates and are subject to margins of error.
- Generative AI tools were used to assist with planning, coding, Vega-Lite specification drafting and explanatory text. The data values are extracted from the cited sources.
